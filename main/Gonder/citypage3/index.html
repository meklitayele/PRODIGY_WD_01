GONDER

History


Gonder is a city in Ethiopia. It is located in the North Gondar Zone of the Amhara Region, north of Lake Tana on the Lesser Angereb River and southwest of the Simien Mountains. As of 2023, Gondar has an estimated population of 487,224. Gondar pr eviously served as the capital of both the Ethiopian Empire and the subsequent Begemder Province.The city is also known for its unique architecture and the annual Timkat festival, which celebrates the Ethiopian Orthodox Epiphany .

Heritage

is a city in Ethiopia that is known for its historical and cultural significance. It was the capital of Ethiopia from 1632 to 1855 and is home to several palaces, castles, and churches that were constructed by a series of emperors from Fasilides to Iyasu II. The ruins of these structures stand within a walled imperial enclosure. The most notable of these structures is the Fasil Ghebbi, a fortified compound consisting of six major building complexes surrounded by a wall 900 meters long. The compound contains some twenty palaces and royal buildings and thirty churches. Handicrafts, painting, literature, and music flourished in the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries. Fasil Ghebbi is included in the World Heritage List.

Facts

  • Gondar was founded in 1636 by Emperor Fasilides and served as the imperial capital of Ethiopia for over two centuries .
  • The Royal Enclosure in Gondar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, encompassing several of the city’s most impressive castles and ruins
  • Gondar is often referred to as the “African Camelot” due to its medieval-like architecture .
  • The city is surrounded by breathtaking natural landscapes, from the Simien Mountains to Lake Tana, offering stunning opportunities for outdoor exploration and adventure .

How To Get To Gondar

By Road

To go from Addis Ababa to Gondar by road, take a bus from Meskel Square Bus Station or hire a private car. The journey takes approximately 8-10 hours, depending on traffic and road conditions.

By Air

To travel from Addis Ababa to Gondar by air, you can book a flight from Bole International Airport in Addis Ababa to Gondar Airport in Gondar. Several airlines, such as Ethiopian Airlines, offer regular flights between the two cities. The flight duration is usually around 1 hour.

By Train

Currently, there is currently no direct train service available from Addis Ababa to Gondar. However, the Ethiopian government has been working on expanding the railway network in the country.
