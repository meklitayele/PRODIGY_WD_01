Experiences

Art & Craft

In Addis Ababa, there are numerous arts and crafts activities to explore. You can engage in traditional weaving, pottery and ceramics, wood carving, jewelry making, painting and drawing, leatherwork, basket weaving, beading and embroidery, as well as recycled crafts. From learning traditional weaving techniques to creating unique pottery, sculptures, jewelry, and leather goods, Addis Ababa offers a vibrant arts scene where you can immerse yourself in Ethiopian culture and express your creativity through various crafts.

Festivals

In Addis Ababa, we have some incredible events and festivals that you should experience during your visit. One of the most captivating festivals is Timket, also known as Epiphany, which happens in January. It's a vibrant celebration of the baptism of Jesus, filled with colorful processions, traditional attire, and lively music. You'll witness the blessing of water and even have the chance to join in the festivities by immersing yourself in the water alongside the locals. Another remarkable festival is Meskel, celebrated in September, which commemorates the discovery of the True Cross. The highlight is the lighting of a massive bonfire called the Demera, accompanied by joyous singing, dancing, and cultural performances. It's a time of spiritual reflection and community bonding. These festivals offer a unique opportunity to immerse yourself in Ethiopian culture, witness captivating traditions, and create unforgettable memories in the vibrant city of Addis Ababa.

Nature

Addis Ababa, offers foreigners a unique opportunity to experience both urban life and natural beauty. Amidst the bustling streets and vibrant culture, there are natural attractions like Entoto Mountain, where hiking to the summit provides breathtaking panoramic views of the city and its surroundings. Additionally, foreigners can explore the lush green spaces of parks and gardens within the city, such as the beautiful Unity Park, where they can enjoy peaceful walks, admire diverse flora, and even spot wildlife. Beyond the city limits, foreigners can venture to nearby destinations like the stunning Great Rift Valley, with its picturesque landscapes, lakes, and opportunities for wildlife encounters. Addis Ababa provides a wonderful balance of urban living and access to nature, allowing foreigners to immerse themselves in the diverse and captivating natural beauty of Ethiopia.

Market

Addis Ababa boasts a vibrant and bustling market scene, offering a rich tapestry of sights, sounds, and flavors for locals and foreigners alike. At the heart of the city lies Merkato, one of the largest open-air markets in Africa. Spanning a vast area, Merkato is a labyrinth of narrow streets and bustling alleys, where you can find an astonishing array of goods, from traditional crafts and textiles to spices, fresh produce, and household items. It's a sensory overload with vibrant colors, enticing aromas, and the lively chatter of vendors and shoppers. Another notable market is Sholla, known for its lively atmosphere and diverse offerings. Here, you can explore stalls selling traditional Ethiopian clothing, intricate jewelry, leather goods, and local handicrafts. Navigating these markets is an adventure in itself, as you haggle for bargains, immerse yourself in the local culture, and discover the vibrant spirit of commerce that permeates the vibrant market scene in Addis Ababa.
